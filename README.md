# Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis
## Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate - Capstone Project

### Project Overview
In this case study, I analyzed over 12 months of historical trip data from Cyclistic, a bike-share company in Chicago. Using Python for data cleaning and Power BI for visualization, I identified key differences in how casual riders and annual members use the service. My analysis revealed that casual riders take longer trips and are most active on weekends. Based on these insights, I proposed three strategic recommendations to convert casual riders into annual members to drive long-term growth.

---

### Key Visual Insights

#### 1. Weekly Trip Distribution
![Weekly Distribution](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/AseelAlnoor/Cyclistic-Bike-Share-Analysis/main/newplot%20(3).png)
**Insight:** Annual members exhibit consistent usage patterns throughout the workweek, aligning with professional commuting. Casual ridership peaks significantly during weekends (Saturday and Sunday), indicating leisure-oriented usage.

#### 2. Average Trip Duration Comparison
![Trip Duration](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/AseelAlnoor/Cyclistic-Bike-Share-Analysis/main/newplot%20(4).png)
**Insight:** There is a substantial disparity in ride durations. Casual riders average approximately 44 minutes per trip, whereas annual members average 16 minutes. This confirms that casual riders utilize the service for longer, recreational journeys.

#### 3. Top 10 Most Popular Routes
![Popular Routes](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/AseelAlnoor/Cyclistic-Bike-Share-Analysis/main/newplot%20(2).png)
**Insight:** High-traffic routes for casual riders are primarily concentrated around tourist landmarks and waterfront areas, while member activity is distributed across commercial and residential transit corridors.

---

### Strategic Recommendations
Based on the data-driven insights, the following marketing strategies are recommended:

1. **Seasonal Membership Promotions:** Implement targeted marketing campaigns during peak summer months at high-traffic casual stations to promote the cost-effectiveness of annual memberships.
2. **Weekend-Specific Incentives:** Develop a "Weekend Pass" or tiered membership structure that appeals to the high volume of casual riders identified during the weekend periods.
3. **Digital Engagement:** Utilize digital targeting at the top casual stations to highlight the benefits of membership for frequent, long-duration riders.
